================================================================================
1. ONE-SENTENCE PITCH
================================================================================
8-bit getaway endless runner: swipe through city / rural / highway roads,
manage police heat by never slowing down too long, and take prompted turns to
change biomes — WebGL-first for mobile browsers, with Unity C# scaffolding for
a later native build.

================================================================================
2. FANTASY & CORE LOOP
================================================================================
You are fleeing. Stay fast. Don’t wreck. Don’t get busted.

Run loop:
  Menu (car parked curbside) → Start Engine → pull-out intro → endless drive
  → collect cash → optional Garage upgrades → die (Wrecked / Busted) → Retry

Session goals:
  - Maximize distance (meters = score)
  - Collect coins ($) for persistent Garage upgrades
  - Survive traffic, wrong-way lanes, red-light gambles, and police heat

Death conditions:
  - Wrecked!  — collide with civilian / police / cross-traffic truck
  - Busted!   — police heat hits 100 → pursuit car catches you from behind

================================================================================
3. CONTROLS (IMPORTANT — NONSTANDARD)
================================================================================
Mobile: swipe on canvas. Desktop: A/D/W/S (or arrows) + Space.

  Swipe LEFT  / A  → move RIGHT one lane   (INVERTED on purpose)
  Swipe RIGHT / D  → move LEFT one lane    (INVERTED on purpose)
  Swipe DOWN  / S  → BRAKE (sticky — stays braked until you resume)
  Swipe UP    / W / Space → resume throttle / cancel brake

During a turn cue (HUD shows ← BIOME · BIOME →):
  Swipe L/R chooses the exit and starts a biome transition corridor.

Why inverted: game fantasy is “steering like you’re looking ahead / drifting”
feel; do not “fix” inversion without intentional redesign.

================================================================================
4. BIOMES & ROAD LAYOUT
================================================================================
Three biomes. Segments are 20 m long along +Z. Player drives +Z.

CITY (default start)
  - 4 lanes, road width 16 m
  - Lane X: -6, -2, +2, +6
  - dirs: [+1, +1, -1, -1]  → left two forward, right two oncoming
  - Double yellow at x=0; white dashes within each half
  - Sidewalks + building boxes
  - Default forward lane index: 1 (x=-2)
  - Dense intersections (~22%)

RURAL
  - 2 lanes, width 10 m
  - Lane X: -2, +2
  - dirs: [+1, -1]  → left forward, right oncoming
  - Grass berms + houses
  - Medium intersections (~14%)

HIGHWAY
  - 2 lanes, width 10 m
  - Lane X: -2, +2
  - dirs: [+1, +1]  → both same direction (one-way)
  - Guardrails + sparse overhead gantries (~every 6 segments)
  - Rare intersections (~6%)
  - Later distance biases turn offers toward highway

WRONG WAY: if player is on a lane with dir=-1, HUD shows "WRONG WAY".
Oncoming traffic still collides — intentional risk/reward for daring lane use.

================================================================================
5. SIGNATURE SYSTEMS (WHAT MAKES IT THIS GAME)
================================================================================

A) POLICE HEAT
  - Heat 0–100 bar on HUD
  - Rising when speed < ~10 OR while braking, after a short grace (~0.6s)
  - Decaying while you stay fast
  - At 100: spawns pursuit police behind you; if they reach you → Busted!
  - Running a red light FAST also dumps +22 heat (and grants boost)

B) STICKY BRAKE
  - Brake is not a momentary tap; it holds until swipe-up / W / Space
  - Brake multiplies target speed by 0.4
  - Used for: surviving reds safely, dodging, or deliberately managing pace
  - Tradeoff: braking builds heat → bust risk

C) TRAFFIC LIGHT RISK / REWARD (intersection segments)
  Lights cycle green → yellow → red.
  When you cross the intersection (~|dz|<4):
    RED + going fast (speed>12, not braking):
      → BOOST 1.35× for 2.5s
      → +22 heat
      → spawn cross-traffic truck sweeping across the road
      → HUD "RED! BOOST"
    RED + braking / slow:
      → safe "RED SLOW" (no boost, no truck)
    YELLOW + fast:
      → +8 heat, "YELLOW!"
    GREEN:
      → "GREEN OK"

D) TURN OFFERS → BIOME TRANSITIONS
  Periodically a turn-offer segment appears (side stubs + chevrons).
  HUD: ← RURAL · HWY → (example)
  Window ~1.25s while near the tile; miss it = continue straight.
  Accepting a turn builds a pooled transition corridor:
      exit ramp (from) → taper (from) → enter ramp (to) → settle ×2 (to)
  Soft lane remap into nearest forward lane of the new biome.
  Cooldown ~10 segments between offers.
  After ~1200 m, highway is weighted more often as a turn option.

E) TRAFFIC
  - Civilians ahead (same direction) and oncoming where biome allows
  - ~12% chance same-direction spawn is police (non-pursuit, still wrecks)
  - Spawn interval tightens with distance
  - Collision AABB-ish: |dz|<2.2 and |dx|<1.35 → wreck

F) COINS + GARAGE
  - Coins spawn on forward lanes (~55% of non-turn segments)
  - Persist in localStorage key: EndlessChase.Save.v1
  - Upgrades (max 5 each), costs 50 / 100 / 200 / 400 / 800:
      SPEED  → +8% top speed per level
      ACCEL  → +10% accel damp rate per level
      HANDL  → snappier lateral spring (smooth time 0.18 → 0.08)

6. ART / PRESENTATION LOCK
================================================================================
Style: Retro NES-like chase — NOT photoreal, NOT GTA, NOT PBR.

  - Limited palette (navy #1D2B53, white #FFF1E8, red #FF004D, orange #FFA300,
    yellow #FFEC27, green #00E436, forest #008751, asphalt #292A32, gray #83769C)
  - Pixel textures, NearestFilter, no mipmaps
  - MeshBasicMaterial / unlit only
  - CRT scanlines + Press Start 2P UI font
  - Internal render historically 320×180 nearest upscale; current portrait buffer
    is 160×256 with FOV 72 so outer city lanes stay on-screen
  - Sky is camera-parented (not a world dome — player would drive through it)
  - Fog navy, short draw distance for mobile

Textures: docs/assets/nes/*.png (CC0 procedural; regenerate via
  node scripts/gen-nes-textures.mjs after npm i pngjs)

Cars: box body + sprite billboards (top + sides), not high-poly meshes.

7. ARCHITECTURE — WHAT IS “THE GAME” TODAY
================================================================================
TWO stacks exist. The SHIPPED / PLAYABLE product is the Three.js WebGL client
under docs/. Unity C# under Assets/Scripts/ is scaffolding for a later iOS /
native port and is NOT what GitHub Pages runs.

── A) PLAYABLE CLIENT (source of truth for gameplay feel) ──
  docs/index.html          UI shells: Menu / HUD / Game Over / Garage
  docs/styles.css          CRT overlay, panels, heat bar, portrait stage
  docs/game.js             Main loop, input, heat, turns, traffic, collisions
  docs/js/constants.js     Tunables, biome layouts, colors, upgrade costs
  docs/js/save.js          localStorage save + upgrade helpers
  docs/js/pool.js          Simple object pool
  docs/js/worldgen.js      Seeded PRNG, segment decisions, transition plans
  docs/js/nes.js           Textures, cars, coins, segment mesh factory
  docs/assets/nes/         Pixel PNGs
  Three.js 0.160 via CDN import map

  Runtime debug hook: window.__endlessChase
    { startRun, setupMenuScene, getSave, getState }

── B) UNITY SCAFFOLD (parallel, not feature-parity) ──
  Assets/Scripts/
    Core/GameBootstrap.cs
    Input/WebSafeTouchInput.cs, WebGlBrowserGuard.cs
    Level/LevelManager.cs, RoadSegment.cs, BiomeDefinition.cs
    Player/LanePlayerController.cs   ← still 3-lane + jump/slide (older model)
    Traffic/TrafficSpawner.cs, TrafficVehicle.cs
    RiskReward/TrafficLightController.cs, IntersectionSegment.cs
    Meta/SaveService.cs, UpgradeManager.cs
    Pooling/ObjectPool.cs, PooledObject.cs
    UI/GameUIController.cs, HUDView.cs, UpgradePanel.cs
  Assets/Shaders/          ToonUnlit + MobilePBR (prefer Unlit for NES look)
  Assets/Plugins/WebGL/    touch-action / preventDefault jslib
  Assets/WebGLTemplates/EndlessChase/

IMPORTANT DIVERGENCE (do not assume Unity matches Web):
  - Web client: 4-lane city bidirectional, sticky brake, heat/bust, turn biomes
  - Unity player: classic 3-lane runner with jump/slide; biome by distance gates
  - When adding features for players, prefer docs/js + docs/game.js first

── C) TOOLING ──
  package.json scripts: serve, smoke, smoke:live (Playwright)
  scripts/smoke.mjs, scripts/gen-nes-textures.mjs
  docs/ArtBible.md, ArtPrompts_*, UITestChecklist.md, WebGLBuildSettings.md

10. WHAT EXISTS VS WHAT DOES NOT (IDEATION GUARDRAILS)
================================================================================
EXISTS
  - Endless procedural road with pooled segments
  - 3 biomes + seamless turn transitions
  - Heat / pursuit bust
  - Sticky brake
  - Red-light NOS gamble + cross traffic
  - Oncoming lanes + wrong-way warning
  - Coins + 3 persistent upgrades
  - Mobile swipe + keyboard
  - NES art pipeline + CRT presentation
  - Attract-mode menu street with parked car + curb pull-out intro

DOES NOT EXIST YET (fertile ground for additions — not commitments)
  - Story / characters / dialogue
  - Multiple player cars or unlockable vehicles
  - Weapon / shooting / combat beyond crash/bust
  - Jump / slide in the Web client (Unity scaffold has stubs only)
  - Multiplayer / leaderboards / accounts
  - Audio / music / SFX (essentially silent)
  - Mission objectives beyond “go far”
  - Power-ups besides red-light boost
  - Day/night cycle, weather, time of day (night palette only)
  - Cops as AI chasers with tactics (only heat→spawn catch-up car)
  - Tutorial beyond tagline
  - Unity/Web feature parity
  - Native iOS store build shipping from this repo today

CONSTRAINTS FOR ADDITIONS
  - Keep mobile WebGL cheap: unlit, pooled, short draw distance, no PBR
  - Preserve NES palette / nearest-neighbor look
  - Prefer extending docs/game.js + docs/js/* for playable changes
  - Portrait-first; don’t assume landscape HUD space
  - Input already inverted for lanes — new gestures must not fight that
  - Heat + brake + red lights are the identity triad; don’t dilute them
    into a generic Subway Surfers clone without reason

================================================================================
12. HOW TO THINK ABOUT CHANGES
================================================================================
When proposing an addition, answer:
  1) Does it reinforce “getaway under heat” or just add clutter?
  2) Can it ship in the Three.js client without new heavy assets?
  3) Does it need a new segment kind (pool key), HUD affordance, or save field?
  4) How does it interact with brake / heat / red / turns?
  5) Mobile one-thumb: is the gesture free or does it conflict with L/R/U/D?

Primary files to touch for most gameplay ideas:
  docs/js/constants.js   (numbers, layouts)
  docs/js/worldgen.js    (spawn rules, biomes)
  docs/js/nes.js         (visuals / segment kinds)
  docs/game.js           (loop, input, systems, UI hooks)
  docs/index.html + styles.css (panels / HUD)

Art bible: docs/ArtBible.md
